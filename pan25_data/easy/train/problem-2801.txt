Filibuster.
The Senate made a rule that instead of having someone actually filibuster (which wastes everyone's time), they can just declare they will filibuster, which will then require a 60 person vote to overrule.
Technically the 60 person vote is to override the filibuster, not the vote on the bill itself (which requires a simple majority).
Budget bills are exempt from any filibuster iirc because otherwise nothing would ever get passed.
