I appreciate pushes for affordable housing and recognize the need to stop vulnerable people from becoming homeless in the first place, and to provide a support system for people who are willing and able to get back on their feet.
A friend of mine who lives in Portland said he just drove his usual route to work and a block of homeless tiny houses behind a wooded fence were no longer there.
During the pandemic, it was pretty common for large groups of homeless to set up encampments on public property and stay camped for months, even years.
Some of our gorgeous parks became trash filled tent cities littered with needles.
The few times I ventured downtown, it looked abandoned with boarded up windows, vandalism, and camping tents everywhere.
